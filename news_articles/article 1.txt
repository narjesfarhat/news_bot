📰 1. Global Markets Shake Amid Fragile Middle East Ceasefire
Global financial markets showed signs of instability as a fragile ceasefire between the United States and Iran failed to reassure investors. Oil prices surged toward $100 per barrel, driven by ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz—one of the world’s most critical oil shipping routes.
Although some vessels have resumed passage, thousands remain delayed as Iran continues to selectively control transit through the region. This uncertainty has fueled volatility across stock markets in the United States, Europe, and Asia.
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) warned that even if tensions ease, the global economy may suffer lasting damage. Growth projections have been downgraded, with U.S. GDP growth for late 2025 revised downward and inflation pressures creeping upward.
Central banks and policymakers are increasingly concerned about prolonged geopolitical instability. Analysts suggest that sustained disruption to oil supply chains could keep energy prices elevated and further strain global economic recovery.
